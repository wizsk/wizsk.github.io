Features that matter

Built for real study sessions, not for checkbox theater.

⌕

Multi-word search

Look up phrases, not just isolated words, so you spend less time fighting the search box and more time learning.

⇱

Reader mode

Tap any word in the text to get a meaning instantly. It is like a polite dictionary interrupting the page.

⌂

Bookmarks

Save useful entries and come back later without remembering where you left that one perfect reference.

◌

Smart suggestions

Helpful suggestions for when your typing is not exactly on speaking terms with your brain.

↯

Fully offline

No internet permission, no network dependency, no surprise usage bill for looking up a root.

⚙

Lightweight & open

About 50 MB compressed database, GPLv3 source, and a build that does not act like a storage warehouse.

Dictionary list

A compact library of the classics, plus a couple of Arabic–English dictionaries for practical lookup.

1

Direct Dictionary (المعجم المباشر)

Arabic → English translation dictionary for fast everyday lookup.

2

Hans Wehr (معجم هانز فير)

The most widely used modern Arabic–English dictionary.

3

Lane Lexicon (معجم لِين)

A highly detailed classical Arabic–English lexicon with extensive citations and nuance.

4

Al-Ghani (المعجم الغني)

Comprehensive Arabic dictionary focused on modern and useful vocabulary.

5

Mukhtar (مختار الصحاح)

Concise classical Arabic dictionary, handy for quick definitions and root lookups.

6

Lisan Al-Arab (لسان العرب)

One of the most comprehensive classical Arabic dictionaries ever compiled.

7

Al-Muashirah (معجم العربية المعاصرة)

Modern Arabic dictionary for contemporary usage and current vocabulary.

8

Al-Waseet (المعجم الوسيط)

A medium-sized modern Arabic dictionary with broad practical coverage.

9

Al-Muhit (القاموس المحيط)

A major comprehensive Arabic dictionary with deep classical coverage.

10

Maqayis al-Lugha (مَقايِيسُ اللُّغَةِ)

Root-based semantic analysis by Ibn Faris (4th century AH).

11

Mufradat Alfaz al-Qur’an (مُفْرَداتُ أَلْفَاظِ القُرْآنِ)

Qur’anic lexicon by al-Raghib al-Isfahani, ideal for Qur’anic vocabulary study.
