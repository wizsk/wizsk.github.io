Your guide to Arabic literature

Here is a curated reading list for anyone who wants to explore Arabic literature.

The books here are selected so that you can easily copy and paste the text into the Arabic Lexicons Reader Input Screen. You can also listen to the audio while reading, as I have included audio links for most of the books. In the beginning, listening helps much more than you might think! Since Arabic is typically written without Harakat (vowels), listening is truly essential to get accustomed to it.

While reading in the app, simply click on any word to instantly see its meaning. Whenever you look up a word, it will automatically be highlighted and saved to your "foreign words" list. This makes it incredibly easy to remember and recognize the word if you encounter it again later in the book! You can also manually bookmark words to reference them at any time.

I hope this helps you!

Arabic Reader App

The companion app built specifically for reading Arabic texts. Open any book and tap a word to get its meanings.

  • Tap any word for its meaning, root, and notes
  • Bookmark words and build your personal vocabulary list
  • Adjust font size and line spacing for comfort
  • Works offline once a book is downloaded

All books listed on مكتبة are compatible with the reader. Look for the "فتح الكتاب" button on each card to open it, then copy the books text and paste it in the app.
